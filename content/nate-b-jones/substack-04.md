# Most of What You're Building Will Be Replaced by a Better Model. Here Are the Five Layers Between You and Irrelevance.
**Date:** April 10, 2026

Companies without the resources of Anthropic, OpenAI, or Google face existential pressure to find defensible market positions as AI models advance rapidly.

The AI app builder category serves as a cautionary example. Lovable raised $330M at a $6.6B valuation and achieved $400M ARR by February, yet the entire category faces collapse. Companies like Lovable, Bolt, Replit, and Shipper function as thin wrappers around the same foundation models with minimal competitive moat — approximately one week of differentiation before superior models erode their value.

The core thesis identifies five structural elements that AI cannot autonomously replace, which will organize the future internet architecture.

**The Five Durable Verticals:**

- **Trust**
- **Context**
- **Distribution**
- **Taste**
- **Liability**

The distinction separates companies like Replit, Vercel, and Notion from wrapper companies facing elimination. These survivors operate at different architectural layers — they don't merely wrap foundation models but provide irreplaceable infrastructure.

The article includes tools for readers: a positioning audit and an "agent-readiness stress test" to determine which vertical suits their business and whether AI agents can meaningfully interact with their product.

Understanding these five layers reveals where enduring value resides in the AI-driven web economy, moving beyond temporary competitive advantages toward structural defensibility.
